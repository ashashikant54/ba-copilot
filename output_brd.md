# Business Requirements Document (BRD)

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Employee Onboarding Portal project aims to streamline and automate the current manual onboarding process for new employees, which is currently reliant on emails and paper forms. By addressing the inefficiencies in the onboarding workflow, the project seeks to enhance the new employee experience and reduce administrative burdens on the HR department.

## 2. BUSINESS PROBLEM
The HR department is currently facing significant challenges with a slow and manual employee onboarding process that takes approximately two weeks. This process involves multiple teams (HR, IT, and Finance) manually processing each new hire, leading to frequent errors and instances where new employees start without the necessary system access. The reliance on outdated methods results in wasted time and resources, negatively impacting both employee satisfaction and operational efficiency.

## 3. BUSINESS GOALS
- Reduce onboarding time from 2 weeks to 2 days.
- Automate IT access provisioning on the first day of employment.
- Provide HR with real-time visibility into the onboarding status of new hires.
- Ensure 100% compliance with document completion.
- Reduce HR manual effort by 60%.

## 4. SCOPE
### In Scope:
- Development of a digital onboarding portal that automates the onboarding workflow.
- Integration with existing HR, IT, and Finance systems for seamless data transfer.
- Implementation of real-time tracking and reporting features for HR.

### Out of Scope:
- Changes to existing HR policies or procedures outside of the onboarding process.
- Development of training materials for new employees beyond the onboarding portal.

## 5. STAKEHOLDERS
| Role            | Name/Team      | Interest                                      |
|-----------------|----------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| HR Director      | HR Department  | Wants faster onboarding and less manual work  |
| IT Manager       | IT Department  | Wants automated access provisioning            |
| Finance          | Finance Team   | Wants accurate cost tracking per new hire     |
| New Employees    | New Hires      | Want a smooth first-day experience            |

## 6. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
FR-001: The system must allow HR to input new employee information electronically.  
FR-002: The system must automate the provisioning of IT access on the first day of employment.  
FR-003: The system must provide real-time visibility into the onboarding status for HR.  
FR-004: The system must ensure that all compliance documents are completed before the employee's start date.  
FR-005: The system must allow for integration with existing HR, IT, and Finance systems.

## 7. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
NFR-001: The system must be user-friendly and intuitive for HR personnel and new employees.  
NFR-002: The system must ensure data security and compliance with relevant regulations.

## 8. USER STORIES
**Story 1:**
- As an HR personnel
- I want to input new employee information electronically
- So that I can reduce manual data entry errors
- Acceptance Criteria: New employee information can be entered and saved without errors.

**Story 2:**
- As an IT Manager
- I want the system to automatically provision IT access on the first day
- So that new employees can start working immediately
- Acceptance Criteria: IT access is granted automatically on the first day of employment.

**Story 3:**
- As a new employee
- I want to have a smooth onboarding experience
- So that I can feel welcomed and prepared on my first day
- Acceptance Criteria: All necessary information and access are provided before the start date.

**Story 4:**
- As an HR Director
- I want to track the onboarding status in real-time
- So that I can ensure compliance and efficiency
- Acceptance Criteria: A dashboard is available showing the onboarding status of all new hires.

## 9. SUCCESS METRICS
- Reduction in onboarding time from 2 weeks to 2 days.
- Percentage of new employees who have IT access on their first day (target 100%).
- Reduction in manual effort for HR by 60%.
- Compliance document completion rate (target 100%).
- Employee satisfaction scores regarding the onboarding experience.

## 10. ASSUMPTIONS & RISKS
### Assumptions:
- Stakeholders will provide timely feedback and support throughout the project.
- Existing systems can be integrated with the new onboarding portal without significant issues.

### Risks:
- Resistance to change from HR personnel accustomed to the current manual process.
- Potential delays in system integration with existing HR, IT, and Finance systems.
