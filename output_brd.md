## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The HR department is currently facing challenges with a slow manual employee onboarding process that relies on emails and paper forms. This inefficiency leads to delays in system access for new employees and consumes a significant amount of HR's time on repetitive tasks, necessitating a digital solution to automate the onboarding process end to end. (Source: [1])

## 2. BUSINESS PROBLEM
The current onboarding process takes approximately two weeks and involves manual processing by HR, IT, and Finance teams, resulting in frequent errors and delays. New hires often begin without the necessary system access, and HR spends about 40% of their time on repetitive onboarding tasks. Additionally, there is a lack of visibility into the onboarding status, and compliance documents are sometimes missed or incorrectly filed. This inefficiency not only impacts new employees' experiences but also strains HR resources. (Source: [1], [2])

## 3. BUSINESS GOALS
- Reduce onboarding time from two weeks to two days.
- Automate IT access provisioning on the first day of employment.
- Provide HR with real-time visibility into the onboarding status of new hires.
- Ensure 100% completion of compliance documents.
- Decrease HR's manual effort in onboarding tasks by 60%. (Source: [3])

## 4. SCOPE
### In Scope:
- Development of a digital onboarding portal.
- Automation of IT access provisioning.
- Integration of compliance document management.

### Out of Scope:
- Changes to existing HR policies and procedures unrelated to onboarding.
- Training for HR staff on new processes (TBC with stakeholders).

## 5. STAKEHOLDERS
| Role          | Team      | Interest                                       |
|---------------|-----------|-----------------------------------------------|
| HR Director   | HR        | Wants faster onboarding and less manual work  |
| IT Manager    | IT        | Wants automated access provisioning            |
| Finance       | Finance   | Wants accurate cost tracking per new hire     |
| New Employees  | Employees | Want a smooth first-day experience            |
(Source: [3])

## 6. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
FR-001: The system shall allow new hires to complete all necessary paperwork digitally.
FR-002: The system shall automate the provisioning of IT access on the first day of employment.
FR-003: The system shall provide real-time tracking of the onboarding status for HR.

## 7. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
NFR-001: The system shall ensure data security and compliance with relevant regulations.
NFR-002: The system shall be user-friendly and accessible to all new hires.

## 8. USER STORIES
**Story 1:**
- As a new employee,
- I want to complete my onboarding paperwork online,
- So that I can start my job without delays.
- Acceptance Criteria: All required forms are available online and can be submitted electronically.

**Story 2:**
- As an HR manager,
- I want to track the onboarding status of new hires in real-time,
- So that I can ensure a smooth onboarding process.
- Acceptance Criteria: The system provides a dashboard with the status of each new hire.

## 9. SUCCESS METRICS
- Reduction in onboarding time from two weeks to two days.
- Percentage of new hires who have IT access on their first day.
- Percentage of compliance documents completed accurately and on time.
- Reduction in HR's time spent on onboarding tasks by 60%.

## 10. ASSUMPTIONS & RISKS
### Assumptions:
- Stakeholders will provide timely feedback during the development process.
- The necessary budget and resources will be allocated for the project.

### Risks:
- Resistance to change from HR staff accustomed to the current manual process.
- Potential technical challenges in integrating the new system with existing HR and IT systems.
